Shifting Themes in Presidential Inaugural Addresses

2025-10-01

Did you know that presidential inauguration address topics have shifted over time? During the world war eras there was a strong focus on peace. In earlier years there was more attention to constitutional rights and civic duty and today more emphasis on national unity and pride.



This figure shows results from a structural topic model (STM) which is a machine learning technique used to identify recurring themes in large collections of text. The model was applied to all US presidential inaugural addresses to uncover major topics and track how their prominence shifted over time. Each bar represents the proportion of a given topic within a single speech with colors indicating themes such as constitutional rights; peace; national security; and national unity. The visualization highlights how the content of inaugural addresses reflects changing historical contexts and national priorities.



Please note that we created these figures to be as accurate as possible, but they have not been formally peer reviewed and should not be considered definitive scholarly work
